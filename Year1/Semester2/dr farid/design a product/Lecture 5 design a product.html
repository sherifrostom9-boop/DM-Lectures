Module 05: Project Management & Acceptance

Closing the Loop: Budgeting, Tenders, and the Professional Pitch to Sponsors

I. Managing the Call for Tenders (Appel d'Offres)

In a real agency, projects don't just "appear." You must fight for them. This module leads students through the process of responding to a client's request for proposal.

1. Analysis of the Ask

Carefully reading the sponsor's needs. What are their hidden "pain points"? What is their real budget?

2. The Financial Bid

Calculating the "cost of production." This includes labor hours, software licenses, and media ad-spend for the international launch.

3. The Competitive Edge

What makes your agency's proposal better than others? (Technical skill? Cultural insight? Legal safety?)

II. Planning & Monitoring Documents

Project management is the art of controlling three variables: **Time, Quality, and Money**. If you change one, the others will shift.

The Essential Dashboard

  • Gantt Chart: Visualizing the timeline. If the filming in Module 04 is delayed, how does it affect the Website Launch in Module 02?
  • Budget Tracking: Monitoring every Euro/Dollar spent to ensure the SME doesn't go bankrupt.
  • Risk Register: Identifying what could go wrong (e.g., technical server failure, legal copyright dispute) and having a Plan B.

III. Presenting the Progress Report

Communication is not just what you *make*, it's how you *sell* it. Students must present their progress to the sponsors (professors/clients).

The "Perfect Pitch" Structure:

  1. The Hook: Remind the client of the problem you solved.
  2. The Demo: Show the showcase site and the creative assets.
  3. The Results: Explain how the international campaign strategy will work.
  4. The Legal Shield: Confirm that all production files and rights are secured.
Dr. Farid's Closing Wisdom: "In a professional context, a project is only successful when the sponsor says 'Yes' to the final delivery. The Procès-Verbal de Recette (Acceptance Report) is your most important document—it signals that you have met your contractual obligations."

IV. The Final Acceptance (La Recette)

The course concludes with the formal "Acceptance of the Project." Students deliver a final report that includes:

  • The live link to the international showcase website.
  • The complete "Production Bible" (Legal and Creative).
  • The final budget vs. initial estimate analysis.
  • A reflection on the "Targeted Competencies" (Design, Express, Develop, Undertake).
