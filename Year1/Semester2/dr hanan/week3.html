Module 03 • Brand Systems

Brand Architecture & Portfolio Strategy

Session Focus

Understanding how organizations structure, manage, and scale multiple brands and digital products while maintaining clarity and equity.

1. Core Architecture Models

Branded House

A single master brand drives all products.

  • Examples: Google, FedEx.
  • Advantage: Lower marketing costs & fast adoption.
  • Risk: Brand damage spreads easily.

House of Brands

Independent brands with no visible link to the parent.

  • Examples: P&G, Unilever.
  • Advantage: Targeted positioning & risk isolation.
  • Risk: High operational costs.

2. The Digital Link

Architecture must be reflected in Information Architecture (IA) and digital ecosystems.

Strategic Implications:
  • Website Structure: Single domain (Branded House) vs. Multiple domains (House of Brands).
  • Domain Strategy: Strategic use of subdomains for SEO discoverability.
  • Social Hierarchy: Deciding between one main account or sub-brand handles.

Activity: The Organizational Chart

Brief: Analyze a conglomerate (Google, Amazon, or Virgin) and map the architecture tree.

Strategic Question:

"If a new digital tool launches, should it inherit the parent identity or require an independent digital ecosystem?"
