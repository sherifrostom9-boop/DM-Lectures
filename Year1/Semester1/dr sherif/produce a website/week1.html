Key Concepts

Human Characteristics

Among the five human senses, vision and hearing are considered to be the most sophisticated due to their substantial representation within the cortical regions of the central nervous system.
In our course: We will concern ourselves with these two senses from a computer systems-based view. We will discuss the methods of representing audio and video in our computer systems.

What is a Digital Ecosystem?

A digital ecosystem is a group of interconnected information technology resources that can function as a unit.
  • Made up of suppliers, customers, trading partners
  • Applications and third-party data service providers
  • All respective technologies

Digital Communication

Digital Communication is the process of exchanging information, messages, and ideas using digital technologies and platforms.

It involves the transmission and reception of data through electronic devices and networks.

Digital Communication enables real-time interaction, instant messaging, video conferencing, and sharing of multimedia content across vast distances.

Data Representation

Data can be anything which represents a specific result or any number, text, image, audio, video, etc.

Forms of data representation: At present, information comes in different forms:

Numbers Text Images Audio Video
The main concern in our course will relate to auditory and visual representation of data.

Analogue Data

An analog signal is any continuous signal for which the time-varying feature (variable) of the signal is a representation of some other time-varying quantity.

In other words, it is analogous to another time-varying signal.

Continuous wave representation with infinite possible values at any given moment.

Digital Data

Computers can only work with digital information. Everything that they process must first be turned into a digital signal in one of two states: 'on' or 'off'.

Discrete representation using binary: 0 (off) or 1 (on)

Binary Terminology

Term Description Size
BIT Binary Digit - the smallest unit of data 1 binary digit (0 or 1)
NIBBLE Half of a byte 4 bits
BYTE Standard unit of data storage 8 bits
REGISTER Small amounts of available storage for specific purposes with quick access time Multiple bytes (group of bits)

Key Concepts

  • Binary: A numbering scheme with only two possible values for each digit: 0 and 1
  • Digit: Any of the numerals from 0 to 9, especially when forming part of a number
  • Nibble: Can be conveniently represented by one hexadecimal digit
  • Digital Processing: All information must be converted to binary (0s and 1s) for computer processing
